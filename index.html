Goals, Projects, and Tasks

A practical reference guide to help George and the team classify work clearly, reduce mental clutter, and move from raw ideas to execution.
Goal / Priority = the business outcome or focus area. Project = the build or initiative. Task = the next action.

1. Why This Guide Exists

  • George has many ideas, notes, and priorities competing for attention.
  • The issue is not lack of ideas. The issue is classification.
  • When goals, projects, and tasks are mixed together, everything feels equally important.
  • This guide helps the team sort George’s raw direction into clear execution levels.
Strategic rule: George should not spend high-value founder time sorting low-level tasks. The team should help convert raw ideas into clean goals, projects, and tasks.

2. Core Definitions

Category Definition Answers This Question Examples
Goal / Priority A business outcome or focus area George wants advanced. It may be broad, strategic, or not yet ready to execute. What result or business area matters?
  • Wholesale Distribution
  • Loyalty Program + Raffle Program System
  • Inventory Management Science
  • File Management System
Strategic Decision A priority area that needs a direction decision before it can become a clear project. What direction must be chosen first?
  • KISS Studio / Bensalem: operate, lease, repurpose, or exit?
  • Branding: internal direction or agency engagement?
Project A temporary, multi-step body of work that creates a defined deliverable in service of a goal or priority. What needs to be built, fixed, launched, or completed?
  • Build Lease Register system
  • Create loyalty rewards structure
  • Set up secret shopper process
  • Build local social media campaign plan
Task A single action with no meaningful sequencing. It supports a project or operating process. What is the next action?
  • Upload lease to folder
  • Email broker
  • Rename file
  • Add project to ClickUp

3. Founder Label Rule

If George identifies something as a goal or priority, we honor it first as a Goal / Priority Area.

The team’s job is not to debate the label. The team’s job is to clarify:
  • What does success look like?
  • Does this need a strategic decision first?
  • What projects sit underneath it?
  • What tasks move the projects forward?

5. Fast Classification Questions

Question If Yes If No
Is this a broad business area George wants advanced? Goal / Priority Go to next question
Does this need a direction decision before work can start? Strategic Decision Go to next question
Does this require multiple steps, sequencing, ownership, and closure? Project Go to next question
Can this be done as one clear action? Task Clarify the work before classifying
Do not ask only: “Is this a goal or project?”

Ask: “Is this the business focus, the decision, the build, or the next action?”

9. Operating Rules for the Team

  • Honor George’s stated goals and priorities first.
  • Do not over-debate labels during planning.
  • Clarify what success looks like by the target date.
  • Identify whether a strategic decision is needed before execution.
  • Convert clear priorities into projects.
  • Classify projects using the Quarterly / Mini / One-off framework.
  • Convert projects into tasks only after the project direction is clear.
  • Do not turn every task into a project.
  • Do not turn every project into a goal.
Management principle: George should provide raw direction. The team should convert it into clean structure before asking for final confirmation.
